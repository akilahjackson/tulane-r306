Who can I contact if I have technical questions or need help in understanding the course?

Technical support

For technical support, such as difficulty accessing any of the site pages, contact TRDirect@tulane.edu or call 504.988.1342. Our business hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM ET, Monday–Friday.

Support

Please use the topics below to troubleshoot any issues you may encounter in this course.

You may also contact any of the groups listed in the Contact Us section for additional help.

How the Course Works

You will find information presented as lecture on the course screens.

This course contains Course Resources, Downloadable Resources and Websites of Interest (open the slide-out Resources tab to access these) and printable notebooks for note taking (open slide-out Notebook tab and select the appropriate link).

Check Your Knowledge

Throughout each lesson there are Check Your Knowledge activities. These activities in the course are designed to help you review important information about the course content and assess your knowledge. These activities can be taken as many times as you like and though scores are not recorded, you will receive real-time feedback as you submit each answer. Consider these activities openbook.

Final Course Exam

After viewing the course content, you must complete a final course exam. A passing grade for the final exam is 70% or higher. You will be provided with unlimited attempts to pass the exam. If you did not successfully complete theexam, please review the course material and try again.

Required Course Survey

After completing the final course exam, you will complete a required survey. We will use your feedback to monitor and improve current and future course offerings.

Certificate of Completion

To receive a Certificate of Completion for this course, you must complete the final exam (you must score 70% or better).

What do I need to know about online courses in order to be successful with them?

Online learning is primarily self-directed. That means that the learners in the online program are responsible for accessing the course on a regular basis, for completing the readings and activities associated with the course without prompting by another person (such as an instructor). Information is primarily delivered over the computer with the learner setting the schedule.

How much time will a course take?

Participants can expect to spend 60-90 minutes per course in this curriculum.

Does this course require a textbook?

There are no textbook requirements for this course.

Are there any required course prerequisites?

No,prerequisites are required.
