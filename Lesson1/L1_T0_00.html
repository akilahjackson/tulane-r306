What is a State Medical Needs Shelter (SMNS) ?

Topic 1

A State Medical Needs Shelter (SMNS) is defined as a shelter of last resort, during emergency conditions for persons requiring limited medical and nursing oversight and who cannot be accommodated in general population shelters.

Local shelters are operated by the Emergency Management Agency (EMA) at the local level in the Mississippi counties.

Regional and state medical needs shelters are operated through the state ESF-8 (public health/MSDH), which operates to provide support for Mississippians with medical needs.

Who is Received into a State Medical Needs Shelter ?

Topic 2

People with access and functional needs are in our communities and will be affected in the event of a disaster. Once a disaster occurs the needs of these residents are often magnified. Considering access and functional needs is an important way to prepare for these residents before a disaster occurs.

Who is Received into a State Medical Needs Shelter ?

Topic 2

Residents who come into a state medical needs shelter may have some type of access and functional needs or medical needs, which may include those:

Acceptance into a State Medical Needs Shelter

Topic 2

  • Minor Health Conditions

    Persons with some type of minor health or medical conditions that require professional observation, assessment or maintenance, who cannot be accommodated by the general population shelter staff or whose needs exceed the ability of the general population shelter to provide service.

  • Chronic Medical Conditions

    Persons with chronic medical conditions, who require assistance with activities of daily living or nursing care but do not need to be hospitalized.

  • Special Medications

    Persons requiring special medications or vital sign readings who require professional observation or assistance.

  • Functional Needs

    Persons with access and functional needs who cannot rely on the general population shelter staff to provide needed care, whether physical, mental or cognitive issues.

SMNS Locations

Topic 3

In Mississippi, state medical needs shelters are located on community college campuses, with the exception of Hudspeth Regional Center and Jackson State University, which is a four-year university.

Mississippi uses community college campuses for state medical needs shelters sites as most of the community colleges have large facilities that can house SMNS populations, have on site food services, access to security personnel, are geographically located in areas that are easily accessible.

SMNS Locations

Topic 3

Some sites also have a separate water treatment system. When the sites were selected, they were installed with back-up generators, HVAC systems, and wireless internet capabilities.

The locations are primarily from interstate 20 and south. If a shelter needs to be opened north of interstate 20, the governor has the authority to give an order to open a medical needs shelter at any state facility.

Stone County SMNS

Topic 3

The Stone County SMNS was opened in 2016. It was built in compliance with current Americans with Disabilities Act guidelines. The shelter is over 23,000 square feet and meets FEMA 361 requirements. The unprecedented facility, located in Wiggins, Mississippi, was designed to house a total between 100-150 patients, caregivers, and staff for a minimum of 72 hours. It was designed to withstand Category 5 hurricane force winds. The shelter has an isolation area for residents who are contagious. It has a kitchen, activity room/commons area, laundry room, treatment room, nurse’s station and a separate area for staff. The shelter has been activated since it’s completion. It was the first stand-alone medical needs shelter of its kind in the United States.
