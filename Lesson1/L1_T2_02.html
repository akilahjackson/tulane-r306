Acceptance into a State Medical Needs Shelter

2 of 2
  1. Persons with some type of minor health or medical conditions that require professional observation, assessment or maintenance, who cannot be accommodated by the general population shelter staff or whose needs exceed the ability of the general population shelter to provide service.
  2. Persons with chronic medical conditions, who require assistance with activities of daily living or nursing care but do not need to be hospitalized.
  3. Persons requiring special medications or vital sign readings who require professional observation or assistance.
  4. Persons with access and functional needs who cannot rely on the general population shelter staff to provide needed care, whether physical, mental or cognitive issues.
