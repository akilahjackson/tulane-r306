Medical Job Responsibilities

1 of 1
SMNS Medical Team

Position Descriptions Slide Deck.

Use the arrows or the slide indicators at the bottom of the slide deck to navigate.

Medical Director

The Medical Director approves medical protocols for the SMNS staff. There are approved emergency care guidelines which are available at the shelter, if needed. The Medical Director can also approve any procedures that are not in existing protocols and is available for consultation regarding any resident issues that arise.

Staff Physician or Nurse Practitioner

A Staff Physician or Nurse Practitioner is a part of the SMNS team and is available for consultation during shelter activation. Residents should be evaluated and assessed after admission to the shelter, to identify medical issues that require interventions. Telehealth may be utilized, as needed, for consultation with physicians concerning emerging resident care issues, when a physician is not on site at the shelter.

Nurse Manager

The Nurse Manager has several job duties when working in theSMNS. There must be ongoing communication between the Facility Manager, the SMNS Coordinator,and the Nurse Manager,for the shelter to run smoothly. In addition to assisting withopening the shelter, including the pre-survey, the Nurse Manager should identify an area for a Nurse’s station and treatment area. Boxes containing medical, respiratory, clerical and over thecounter medications will be delivered to the shelter for use during activation.

The Nurse Manager must ensure that potential residents are triaged and assessed for admittance into the SMNSand if admitted, are properly locatedwithin the shelter. Certaintypes of residents may require specific placement in the shelter.Those not needing admittance to the SMNSare referred to the correct facility. A tracking record should be maintained for those not admitted to the SMNSand where they were referred to. The Nurse Manager also supervises, ensuring protocols and procedures are followed and that appropriate forms, such as the health surveillance form is filled out every 24 hours and submitted to the epidemiology department.

Other duties of the Nurse Manager include providing support to the nursing staff. As working in a shelter can be stressful on both residents and staff, some may have a stress reaction or psychological shock. The Nurse Manager should observe for such signs and symptoms and should conduct a shift change report with the medical staff, working with the Facility Manager to fill out a situation report. A situation report is a brief summary of important issuesor changes that occurred during the shift, which might include the number of admissions, discharges, incidents, falls or unusual events that happened during the shift. The report is submitted twice a day at 5AM and 5PM to the SMNS Coordinator.

Nursing Staff

Nurses assigned to triage will determine if a potential resident should be admitted to the SMNS, to a general population shelter or to a higher level of care, such as a hospital. Persons not meeting the criteria for the state medical needs shelter should be assisted in a referral to appropriate shelter, facility, hospital or location. A list of those persons not admitted to the SMNS should be kept, with the location of referral documented. The list will be used to maintain information about the referral and provide the information to appropriate persons. After an assessment is done, appropriate placement of the potential resident will be determined. Appropriate patients will be admitted to the SMNS. Residents may have a caregiver who administers medication or may require nurses to assist with medication administration. Only one person/caregiver is allowed admittance to the SMNS.

Other nursing duties are to keep the medical documentation and forms up to date, charting the initial assessment and other nursing interventions. Nurses should also keep the Nurse Manager aware of changes in residents’ conditions.

Nursing Aides

Nursing Aides, under the direction of the Nurse and Nurse Manager should assist residents throughout their shift with personal hygiene,activities of daily livingand other needs that may arise.They should help with transfers, ambulation ofresidents and assistanceduring mealtime. NursingAides can help alleviate the fearsand concerns of residents in the shelter, by providing emotional support, conversation,and diversionalactivities. NursingAides should report any concerns regarding residents to the nurse.

Telemedicine

Within the shelter, thereisaccess to University of Mississippi Medical Center (UMMC)telemedicine, allowing access with physicians, available via webcam 24 hours daily to answer questions, consultations and provide support to residentsin the shelter.

What do I need to know about online courses in order to be successful with them?

Online learning is primarily self-directed. That means that the learners in the online program are responsible for accessing the course on a regular basis, for completing the readings and activities associated with the course without prompting by another person (such as an instructor). Information is primarily delivered over the computer with the learner setting the schedule.

How much time will a course take?
