Non-Medical Job Responsibilities

1 of 1
SMNS Non-Medical Team

Position Descriptions Slide Deck.

Use the arrows or the slide indicators at the bottom of the slide deck to navigate.

Facilty Manager

The Facility Manager is responsible for the non-medical operations of the shelter. Duties include the pre and post survey of the facility, taking inventory for non-medical supplies, setting up registration and waiting areas and notifying the SMNS Coordinator when the shelter has opened. As the Facility Manager has many duties, it is recommended that someone be delegated to serve as the information officer, who will provide accurate information to the residents and caregivers. The Facility Manager will need to collaborate with the College Facility Representative to establish a feeding schedule.

Logistics Manager & Staff

The Logistics Manager receives and keeps a recorded inventory of supplies used or ordered. Logistics Staff duties include keeping the area organized, cleaning, charging radios, computers, and batteries and ensuring theyarein working order. The LogisticManager’smain responsibility is to maintain inventory of supplies/equipment and keep a distribution record of these supplies. This is necessary to document for reimbursement purposes after the shelter is closed. All unused supplies and equipment must be counted and returned. If additional supplies are needed, the LogisticsManagerinforms the SMNS Coordinator. The LogisticsManageralso participates in the shift report and assists with meal feeding schedules.

Clerical & Registration Staff

Clerical Staff havesome initial duties such as when a resident is admitted to the shelter, a chart iscreated to record all the resident’s information and ongoing documentation. As residents enter, Registration and Clerical Staff should assist them with registration and obtaining a history of the resident. A short orientation to the shelter, notifying residents and caregivers of the location of specifically assigned areas, such as restrooms or information areas, would be helpful. All residents and caregivers should be provided with a copy of the shelter rules.

Ongoing actions of Clerical Staff include keeping a record of every person who enters the shelter, whether staff, resident, caregiver, visitor,or any other person. Clerical Staff should keep the registration/clerical area clean and all records/paperwork organized. Information should be provided to residents and caregivers as needed. Information on the shelter residents and other appropriate messages shouldbe reported to the Facility Manager and Nurse Manager, which will then be passed on to the SMNS Coordinator. Keeping an accurate census of residents, caregivers,and staff enables notification to the Facility and Nurse managers when the shelter is at 80% capacity. Clerical staff also participate in shift reports.

Social Worker

The Social Worker position in the shelter is important to all staff, residents,and caregivers. Discharge plans should begin for residents as soon as they are admitted to the shelter. It is necessary to determine whether they will be able to return to their home, if they will need alternate housing,or if a referral for care at a different facility should be implemented. Discharge planning does not need to wait until the day the shelter closes, since it will take time to find housing for residents. Planning should include where they are going, safety, if any referrals are needed,and how they are being transported, which may be by personal vehicle or ambulance. Residents may need assistance with medications, referrals, appointments, housing,or other problems. It is important to determine if the resident has a safe mode of transportation or if arrangements are required. Social Workers collaborate with the Nurse Manager to accommodate appropriate discharge planning. They participate in shift reports and help to keep the shelter clean.

Environmentalist

Environmentalists are responsible for assessing sheltersitesutilizingthe environmental form. Other duties include monitoring all food handling, how food is served, where it is served, proper hygiene monitoring,and correct disposal of waste. Any issues should be reported to the Facility Manager who will report to the SMNS Coordinator.

Safety & Security Staff

While the shelter is open, campus police provide security for the SMNS. Safety& Security Staff function both inside and outsideof the shelter, watching for safety issues that may arise. Any concerns are reported to the Facility Manager and a resolution is attempted. Safety Officersare present in the triage area while residents are being assessed to see if they will be admitted to the SMNSand monitor exits and restricted areas. One main entrance for residents should be established and kept clean, clear,and accessible. The safety of all staff, residents and caregivers is the top concern for all SMNS.One responsibility of the Safety Officer is to assist with placing proper signage at shelter sites, both in and outside the shelter,to identify specific areas, restricted areas, direct the flow of traffic to the shelter,and to increase safety for residents, caregivers,and staff.

Public Information Officer

The MSDH assigns a Public Information Officer (PIO) who handles all information going out to the media/public. Depending on the number of residents, someone may be assigned within the shelter to provide information to the residents, caregivers and staff as needed. No information is provided to the public or the media from any shelter staff members.
