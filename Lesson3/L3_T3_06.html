Resident Referrals

6 of 6

A resident may arrive at the shelter who should be cared for in a hospital setting or their condition may change during their stay at the SMNS, requiring transfer to a higher level of care. If a resident is going to a hospital or another facility, the nurse manager should contact the SMNS coordinator to assist with coordinating the referral and transfer. A copy of the referral and the resident’s record should accompany the patient when transferring. If the weather is too dangerous for travel, no potential resident will be refused entry to the SMNS. Arrangements will be made for transfer to the appropriate facility once the weather improves and is safe for travel, if the person needs hospitalization or transfer to a general population shelter.

Patients Requiring Hospitalization

Even though some people do well at home, with appropriate medical equipment, they may not function as well in a disaster situation. If a person is on a ventilator, requires continuous IV therapy, is eight months pregnant or greater and/or having contractions (regardless of the expected delivery date) or is an unstable oxygen dependent patient they will be referred for transport to the hospital. Though current designated shelter sites have backup generators, the possibility of unrestored power outage must be considered. Therefore, residents requiring a constant power source for equipment will also require hospitalization. Persons with chest pain, who have had chest pain in the last 24 hours or are experiencing shortness of breath should be hospitalized. An ambulance will be on site at the SMNS, once it is activated and for the duration of activation, for assistance with transporting residents to the hospital when needed. State Medical Needs Shelters are not intended to serve as skilled healthcare facilities.

For persons experiencing mental health or behavioral health issues, a request may be made by the SMNS Coordinator for the Mississippi Department of Mental Health to provide support services to the residents, caregivers or staff in the SMNS.
