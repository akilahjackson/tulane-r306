Resident Referrals

6 of 6

A resident may arrive at the shelter who should be cared for in a hospital setting or their condition may change during their stay at the SMNS, requiring transfer to a higher level of care.

Patients Requiring Hospitalization, if they :

  • Require appropriatemedical equipment or a constantpower source
  • Are experiencing chest pain
  • Are experiencing mental health or behavioral health issues

If a resident is going to a hospital or another facility, the Nurse Manager should contact the SMNS Coordinator to assist with coordinating the referral and transfer. A copy of the referral and the resident’s record should accompany the patient when transferring.

An ambulance will be on site at the SMNS, once it is activatedand for the duration of activation, for assistance with transporting residents tothe hospital when needed.SMNSare not intended to serve as skilled healthcare facilities.If the weather is too dangerous for travel, no potential resident will be refused entry to the SMNS. Arrangements will be made for transfer to the appropriate facility once the weather improves and is safe for travelif the person needs hospitalization or transfer to a general population shelter.

Even though some people do well at home,with appropriate medical equipment, they may not function as well in a disaster situation. If a person is on a ventilator, requirescontinuous IV therapy, is eight months pregnant or greater and/or having contractions (regardless of the expected delivery date,) or is an unstable oxygen dependent patient they will be referred for transport to the hospital.

Persons with chest pain, who havehad chest pain in the last 24 hours or are experiencingshortness of breath should be hospitalized.

For persons experiencing mental or behavioral health issues, a request may be made by the SMNS Coordinator for the Mississippi Department of Mental Health (MDMN) to provide support services to the residents, caregivers,or staff in the SMNS
