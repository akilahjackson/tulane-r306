Form 1 – Environmental Assessment Form

The EnvironmentalAssessment Form will be usedto identify any critical needs or recommended changes regarding the facility, food and water, sanitation, solid waste, safety, sleeping, childcare and service animals. Environmental staff is responsible for this assessment ofthe facility.

Form 2 – Facility Survey Form

The Facility Survey Form is used to perform both Pre/Post shelter use, for documentation of any obvious areas of damage prior to facility use, as well as damage to the facility during use. The Facility Manager, Nurse Manager,and Facility Representative are responsible for conducting this survey and completing this form both before and after the shelter is opened and closed.

Form 6 – Intake Assessment Form

The Intake Assessment Form is used to triage potential shelter residents. It is used to assess and identify whether someone meets criteria for admission to SMNS or if they should be sent to a general population shelter or hospital. Professional judgment must be utilized in making the decision of whether someone meets criteria for admittance to SMNS. This form should only be used as a guide.

Form 6 – Intake Assessment Form Page 2
Form 7 – Resident Registration Form

The Resident Registration Form must be completed for every resident admitted to the shelter. It is placed in the front of the residents’ records. This provides important information concerning residents and assists in addressing their medical needs. It includes general personal information, as well as emergency contact information, medical history/information and equipment/supplies brought to the shelter by the resident. This form is used by Clerical Staff, Nurses, Physicians, and Social Workers. There is ademographics section which includes vital signs, allergies, medications, and discharge planning.

Form 8 – Admissions/Daily Census Form

The Admissions/Daily Census Form is maintained at the registration area. Preferably, there will be a designated staff member assigned to track a person’s coming into or leaving the shelter. It should be noted that there is a column on the form for the destination when a resident is transferred or discharged. The Facility Manager is responsible for this report. It is turned in weekly during shelter operations. This report is kept daily and totaled weekly at the end of the week to turn in.

Form 10 – Visitor Sign-In Form

The Visitor Sign-In Form must be completed for anyone coming into the shelter to visit residents. The form includes information concerning the purpose for the visit, arrival, and departure times from the SMNS. This information is required for the safety and security of residents, caregivers, and staff of the SMNS. It is maintained in the clerical area of the shelter. The Facility and Nurse Managers should be made aware of any visitors on the SMNS premises. All visitors must wait in an area that does not interfere with SMNS operations.

Form 11 – Medical Update Form

The Medical Update form is similar to a progress note, where any treatments, observations, medication administrations or issues can be documented concerning the resident. Any time interventions are performed they should be documented on the medical update form.

Form 12 – Surveillance Worksheet Form

The Surveillance Worksheet Form is completed by the nurse manager ending at 7 PM each day and covers the previous 24-hour time period. The form includes information about where the report should be sent. The form records acute onset syndromes or conditions such as respiratory or flu like illnesses, gastrointestinal Illness including diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal cramps or nausea, neurological changes, including severe headaches, migraines, stiff neck, altered mental status, acute delirium, muscle weakness or paralysis, rash illnesses including rashes and/or hives, fever or other skin lesions, particularly acute wound infections or acute onset of symptoms or syndromes not included on the form. Also included on the form are age ranges for residents presenting with any of these issues. Any reported conditions will be reported by the Nurse Manager to the MSDH Epidemiology Office and to the SMNS Coordinator.

Form 13 – Incident Report Form

If an accident occurs, either with or without injury, a violent situation, medication errors or any other incidents occur, an incident report form should be completed. Once completed the form is to be given to the appropriate manager. All incidents are then reported to the SMNS Ccoordinator to determine if further actions are required. A copy of the Incident Report Form should be maintained by the Facility Manager for future reference.

Form 14 - Medical Transfer Form

The Medical Transfer Form is completed for residents who are referred and are being transferred to another facility. The original copy should remain at the SMNS while a copy of the completed form, along with a copy of the resident’s record, should be sent with the resident to the receiving facility.

Discharge Planning Form

A Discharge Planning Form addresses an important checklist of information to consider when discharge planning. It includes where residents will go, how they will be transported, if they need a caregiver when they return home, if it is safe to return home, if power and utilities have been restored and if someone is able to come pick them up. Resources are available through HUD to utilize when planning for discharge if homes have been destroyed or residents are unable to return to their home. It is the goal of SMNS to return residents to the same level of independent living they observed before the emergency, whenever possible.

214 Form

This form correlates to the time you sign in and out as well as your work assignments. The form is to be filled out individually and should capture activities and duties performed during the shift. This form may be used by the facility and nurse managers for the situation report, which is due at 5 AM and 5 PM daily. Each staff member should retain a copy of the 214 form for their own records as well.
